Hi all,

I think these comments are very interesting...

In fact, the way RAT was developed was and is a bit particular: If one of the 
developers needed something for his work, it was added. Consequently, many 
things were never addressed (like the ASAR-AP mode for example). And, of 
course, there is a big lack of documentation.

Therefore I think that a 'knowledge base' could be very helpful, and also a 
comparison of the functionality of the different free and commercial tools is 
surely very interesting. The question is only how to do it? And who is 
willing to help? And what we (the maintainers/developers of RAT) can do to 
support these activities? I am curious to hear your thoughts... The main 
problem is that the development team is very small and very busy in their 
jobs. Some help concerning documentation / wiki / knowledge base would really 
be appreciated. 

Finally: ASAR AP mode data are not really polarimetric and somehow a joke. RAT 
cannot do a lot with them except of maybe displaying them. I recommend to use 
some other tools, but I recommend even more to use other data  ;-)

Regards,
Andreas
