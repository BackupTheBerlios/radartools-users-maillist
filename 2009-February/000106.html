Hi,

Yes, IDIOT is calculating a synthetic interferogram from the SRTM DEM and the 
orbit data. Then, the slave image is coregistered to the master based on the 
orbit data and the DEM, and the real interferogram is calculated. As an 
output you get this plus the difference between real and synthetic one.

The estimation of terrain motion you get by counting the fringes. Each fringe 
corresponds to 2.8cm of motion (half the wavelength of ENVISAT) in 
line-of-sight direction. To estimate horizonal and/or vertical components is 
difficult, you have to combine several interferograms from different 
directions (there are some publications on this topic).
