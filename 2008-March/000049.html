Hi Dave,

With RAT you can do Wishart classification. The routines are basically 
identical to PolSARPro, except that they're not supervised. We just never 
implemented some tools for selecting the training regions (the rest is 
trivial). I don't really know what classical special unitary transforms 
should mean in the context of Wishart classification. Wishart is just the 
statistical distribution covariances follow, and when classifying, you search 
for each pixels the nearest class centre according to this distribution. 
There are no unitary transforms involved. But maybe I have misunderstood you?

A tip: Have a look at the EM-PLR classifyer. It is unsupervised but includes 
some spatial homogenisation.

But generally I agree, we should implement some supervised routines, but I 
fear that as usual anybody has time. Who wants to help?
