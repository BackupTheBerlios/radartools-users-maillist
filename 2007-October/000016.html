GREAT!

Anyway, during the morning a colleague suggested to give a look at the 
EV-APP manual in order to deepen the problem:
in the first pages, when presenting the software, it is clearly stated 
that..."further processing is applied which transforms the SAR image to an 
orientation similar to a photographic image, making the information easier 
to work with for typical interpreter"... and again ..."The image may also be 
oriented at this stage so as to make the top, left-hand pixel the most 
north-west pixel in the image"...

This is the case for descending data (the one you processed) but, we think, 
probably this problem does not appear when dealing with ascending data, as 
the upper (azimuth) direction coincides with north and the right (range) 
direction coincides with east (as in a photographic image). What do you 
think about it?

This should be also the reason why I didn't get problems when processing 
ascending data, do you remember?

So it means that I should use your new idiot version when processing 
descending data and the old one when dealing with ascending data?

Coming to the results, you got very good results: you had about two fringes, 
and in that time span we measured about 4 cm displacement (uplift) from GPS 
and levelling data in the area (Phlegrean Fields).

Thank you really so much!
Sven.
