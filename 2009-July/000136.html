Hi,

Yes, for PolInSAR you need two data sets which are coregistrated. In RAT, you'll need to do InSAR first on each pair of polarisation (HH1-HH2, VV1-VV2, etc.). This mean to split the data into the different channels, combine monopolar combinations as InSAR pairs, coregister them, split them again to save the coregistered slave, and to recombine the 4 coregistered slave channels again into a PolSAR vector.

I know that this is very very complicated and also a bit time consuming. But up to now, nobody implemented a better approach....
